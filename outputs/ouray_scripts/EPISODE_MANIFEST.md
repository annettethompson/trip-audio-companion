## Episode Architecture Notes

**Primary geological focus:** Ice Age glaciation (Ch. 01, new to series), iron-sulfide chemistry at Red Mountain Pass (Ch. 03, new to series; explicitly distinguished from Cutler hematite covered in Day 2), Million Dollar Highway road geology and engineering (Ch. 02), Precambrian schist at Box Canyon Falls (Ch. 04), Silverton caldera ore geology (Ch. 05). Bob-forward throughout.

**Annette anchor chapters:** Ch. 06 (altitude physiology at 11,018 ft: alveolar gas equations, oxyhemoglobin dissociation curve, 2,3-DPG mechanism, UV at altitude), Ch. 07 (alpine wildflowers: glacier lily, sky pilot, Indian paintbrush, shooting star, treeline ecology, Krummholz), Ch. 08 (pika as climate sentinel species, with full physiology).

**Wildlife anchors:** Ch. 08 covers pika (thermoregulation failure, haypiling behavior, climate indicator status), yellow-bellied marmot (hibernation physiology, colony sentry behavior), white-tailed ptarmigan (camouflage, seasonal plumage), golden eagle (soaring, territory scale).

**1923 moment:** Ch. 10 — First automobiles on Red Mountain Pass. Centers on the horse-vs-automobile instinct problem on mountain switchbacks, documented driver panic, the Colorado State Highway Commission accident data, and the Harvey Thornton fictional composite letter. Carries the specific emotional texture of the transition from animal to machine in extreme terrain.

**Come Back chapter compliance (Rule 8):** Ch. 12 contains no new wildlife biology or historical context.
