## Episode Architecture Notes

**Primary geological focus:** San Juan Basin geology as the geological opposite of the volcanic San Juans (Ch. 03, Bob's anchor chapter), Cretaceous Western Interior Seaway, Fruitland Formation coal beds, Animas Canyon rock transition readable from the road (Ch. 04). No repeat of volcanic field content from Day 2.

**Annette anchor chapters:** Ch. 08 (ponderosa pine: vanilla/butterscotch terpene chemistry, fire ecology, park-like old-growth structure), Ch. 07 (Durango Hot Springs: 32-mineral chemistry, facility contrast with Orvis, AquaGen system, Animas River setting), Ch. 10 (Animas riparian ecology: beaver, macroinvertebrate water quality indicators, Gold King spill 2015 and recovery).

**History anchors:** Ch. 01 (D&SNG full story, the home chapter -- only teased in Day 3), Ch. 02 (Durango as planned railroad/smelter city 1880), Ch. 05 (Southern Ute people, distinct from Tabeguache covered Day 2, negotiated reservation retention), Ch. 06 (Fort Lewis: Army fort to Indian boarding school to Fort Lewis College, 1911 free-tuition mandate).

**Wildlife anchors:** Ch. 09 covers elk (San Juan herd, late May calving, bulls in velvet), black bear (spring walking hibernation, hunger season), cliff swallow (mud pellet nest construction, 900-1,200 pellets per nest, colonial nesting), pinon jay (obligate relationship with pinon pine, seed dispersal), Townsend's solitaire (winter juniper specialist). American dipper referenced only as one sentence (home chapter: Day 1).

**1923 moment:** Ch. 11 -- Boarding the K-28 in 1923. The train as the only reliable way in or out of Silverton. Locomotive 473, brand new in 1923, still running today. Full sensory reconstruction: coal smoke, narrow coaches, ledge cuts above the Animas gorge, the 3-4 hour journey. Passenger mix: miners, merchants, early tourists. Silverton's economic context in 1923 (30 years past the silver boom peak).

**Mesa Verde:** geology explained in Ch. 03 (Cretaceous stratigraphy, Cliff House Sandstone, Mancos Shale, alcove formation); practical visit information in Ch. 12 (Come Back). Rule compliant: Ch. 12 does not re-explain geology.
