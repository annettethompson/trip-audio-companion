## Episode Architecture Notes

**Primary geological focus:** San Juan Volcanic Field, caldera eruptions, ignimbrite, ore formation. Bob-forward geology throughout.

**Annette anchor chapters:** 08 (plants: sagebrush, Gambel oak, high-desert ecology), 05 (Orvis hot springs specific chemistry), 09 (wildlife: bighorn, osprey, prairie dogs).

**1923 moment:** Chapter 11 — The Last of the Pack Strings. Centers on the Rio Grande Southern Railroad's economic decline in 1923, the mule packer culture, and the 42-year proximity of the Ute removal to the ranching world the Duttons inhabit.

**No Day 1 repeat content:** Chapter 01 does not re-explain Colorado tectonics, Laramide Orogeny, general geothermal gradient, or basic hot spring physiology. Chapter 05 skips vasodilation/cardiac output basics and focuses on Orvis's specific lithium-dominant water chemistry and multi-pool contrast cycling. Chapter 08 skips willow/cottonwood salicylate chemistry and focuses on what is new: sagebrush, Gambel oak, high-desert plant palette.
